Stephanie Ruhle Early Life:

Stephanie Ruhle was born on 24 December 1975.  She was born to the parents Frank and Louise Ruhle. Stephanie was raised in Park Ridge, New Jersey.  She enrolled herself in Lehigh University from where she attained her bachelor’s degree in International Business in 1997.  She also studied in Guatemala, Italy, and Kenya in pursuit of her major.

Stephanie Ruhle Career:

Stephanie Ruhle started her career at Bloomberg. She served a period of 14 years in the finance sector. While in college, she also did an internship for Merrill Lynch.  She also worked for Credit Suisse in 1997. She shared the responsibility of the Vice President and became the highest credit derivatives salesperson all over the USA.  Moreover, she also worked for Deutsche Bank in the position of credit salesperson.

In addition, Stephanie Ruhle found the Global Market Women’s Network in order to help women get hired for leadership roles in the company.

She hosted an early morning program named ‘Inside Track’. She worked along with the anchor Eric Schatzker. The program was broadcasted on Bloomberg television.  She also joined Markert Makers,  a two-hour late-morning program.

In 2015, Stephanie Ruhle produced and hosted Haiti: Open For Business (2015). The documentary covers the story of Haiti’s emerging markets after the place was devastated by an earthquake.

In addition, Ruhle is a columnist for shape magazine. Moreover, she was featured on the cover of Working Mother Magazine in October 2012. Moreover, Ruhle also found the Corporate Investment Bank Women’s Network. She was also a member of the Women On-wall Street (WOWS) steering committee. She is a part of many projects related to Women’s empowerment.
