Early Life

Timothy MacKenzie Gunn was born in Washington, D.C., on July 29, 1953, to his parents Nancy and George William Gunn. His father was an FBI agent.

He went to the Corcoran College of Art and Design, where he received a BFA in sculpture. He was also very good in swimming and won various tournaments throughout high school.

During his teenage years, he identified himself as gay, although he was raised in an intensely homophobic household environment. When Gunn was 17 years old, he tried to commit suicide by swallowing more than 100 pills.

Until his early 20s, he always denied his sexual orientation to his family. When he was 29, he finally came out to his sister.

Career

Tim Gunn started his career working as director of admissions at Corcoran. He was the associate dean at the Parsons from 1989 to 2000, and later, he became the chairperson Fashion Design Department.

In 2004, Gunn started working with the American reality television series Project Runway. The show ran for a total of 16 seasons, which made Tim Gunn very popular in the US.

In 2007, he came up with another reality show Tim Gunn’s Guide to Style, in which Gunn and his co-host offer a makeover to the guests who appeared on the show.

Gunn also hosted another show, “Under the Gunn,” which aired on television on January 16, 2014. He became the chief creative officer of Liz Claiborne, Inc in the year 2007.

In the same year, Tim Gunn released his first book, A Guide to Quality, Taste, and Style, which was co-written by Kate Moloney. Later, he published four other books from 2010 to 2015.

He also made guest appearances on many television shows, including The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson’s and How I Met Your Mother, where he played the role of Barney’s personal tailor.

Awards & Achievements

In 2013, Tim Gunn received the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Host For A Reality-Competition. On April 27, 2007, he visited Palm Springs, California. The nearby city of Palm Desert honored Gunn with an official resolution, which declares his day of the visit as “Timothy M. Gunn Day.”

The city of Palm Springs also awarded him with a certificate and a plaque in recognition of his career achievements. In 2009, he also received an honorary doctorate from the Corcoran College of Art and Design
