Who is Dr. James Andrews? | Dr. James Andrews Bio and Wiki

James Andrews born on May 2, 1942, is an American orthopedic surgeon. He is a surgeon for shoulder, elbow, and knee injuries and also a specialist in repairing damaged ligaments. Practicing in Alabama, Andrews has become one of the most well known and popular orthopedic surgeons and has performed on many high-profile athletes.

Additionally, he is the team doctor for the Alabama Crimson Tide, Tampa Bay Rays, Auburn University Tigers, and Washington Redskins.

Dr. James Andrews Education

Andrews is a highly educated and qualified person. He received his undergraduate and medical degrees from Louisiana State University, where he was an athlete, winning a Southeastern Conference Championship in pole-vaulting. Andrews completed his residency at Tulane Medical School and completed fellowships at the University of Virginia School of Medicine and the University of Lyon.

Dr. James Andrews Orthopedic Surgeon

Andrews is known for performing orthopedic surgery on high-profile athletes from a wide array of sports and is one of the central figures in sports-medicine.

He has co-founded or chaired multiple research bodies and medical centers, including the Andrews Sports Medicine and Orthopedic Center in Birmingham, Alabama, the American Sports Medicine Institute (ASMI), the Andrews Institute in Gulf Breeze, Florida, and the Andrews Research and Education Foundation. Dr. James Andrews Patients

He has performed surgery on many high-profile athletes. Andrews first became known among athletes when Roger Clemens’ agent advised the pitcher to visit Andrews in 1985. Andrews provided a second opinion to team doctors, performed successful surgery on a torn labrum, and Clemens made a full recovery. He would gain a reputation as an “athlete-centric” doctor, performing on athletes including Jack Nicklaus, John Smoltz, Brett Favre, Bo Jackson, Michael Jordan, and Adrian Peterson.
