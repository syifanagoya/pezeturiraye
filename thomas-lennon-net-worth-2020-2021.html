Early Life & Biography

Thomas Lennon was born on August 9, 1970, to Timothy Lennon and Kathleen Lennon. His full name is Timothy Patrick Lennon. His place of birth was Oak Park in Illinois. His descent is a combination of that of Scottish as well as Irish.

As far as education is concerned, he studied at Oak Park and River Forest High School, where he graduated from back in 1988. He was only 16 years old when Thomas met Kerri Kenny. The two were attending a Theatre Camp held at Northwestern University.

Who would have known that the two would later become great friends and co-stars? Surprisingly, they even joined the same university, New Your University. While there, the two also became members of The New Group, which was a comedy troupe associated with the university.

Career

His acting career began at the university. As far as acting and performing are concerned, he also made appearances on the likes of Viva Variety and Reno 911.

He made guest appearances in the likes of The League, Party Down, Children’s Hospital, New Girl, Comedy Bang Bang, How I Met Your Mother, Don’t Trust the B—- in Apartment 23 and last but not least Friends. Other appearances include The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson, The Daily Show, Jimmy Kimmel Live!, The Tonight Show and Late Night with Jimmy Fallon, among others.

He is also the writer of Bay Watch, The Machine, Hell Baby, Let’s Go to Prison, The Pacifier, and the Night at the Museum films. In 2019, he was himself in an episode of Ryan Hansen Solves Crimes on Television.

As a novelist, Thomas Lennon has written the likes of Ronan Boyle and the Swamp of Certain Death and Ronan Boyle and the Bridge of Riddles. The two earned a place in the list of New York Times Bestsellers as soon as they were released.
